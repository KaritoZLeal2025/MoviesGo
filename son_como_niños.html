Grown Ups

The death of their childhood basketball coach brings together a group of old friends, who reunite at the place where they once celebrated a championship. As they catch up on their wives and kids, they realize that age does not necessarily come with maturity.

REVIEWS

Roger Ebert, Chicago Sun-Times

"Grown Ups is cliché, predictable, and full of immature and juvenile humor. The film recycles too many jokes and quickly becomes boring."

Matt Joseph, We Got This Covered

"Grown Ups is one of Adam Sandler's most successful productions."

Antonio Díaz de León Juárez, El Humanista

"Dumb, lazy, obvious, and largely pointless."

Bill Goodykoontz, Arizona Republic

"Don’t take it too seriously, and you’ll have a good time."
